Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam

He played a crucial role in the development of India's first satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III)

Here's a time line of Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam life's

1931 - Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India

1950 - Graduates from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, with a degree in physics

1957 - Joins the Indian Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) as a scientist

1963 - Becomes project director of India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III)

1969 - Successfully launches SLV-III, making India the sixth country to launch a satellite into orbit

1982 - Appointed as chief executive of the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO)

1992 - Becomes the scientific advisor to the Indian Prime Minister and secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO)

1998 - Successfully conducts nuclear tests in Pokhran, Rajasthan

2002 - Elected as the 11th President of India

2007 - Leaves office and becomes a visiting professor at various universities

2015 - Passes away in Shillong, Meghalaya

Additional Information:
  • Kalam was a prolific writer and published several books on science, technology, and leadership.
  • He was awarded many honors and awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor.
  • He is considered one of the most influential and respected figures in India's history.
